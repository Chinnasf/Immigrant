# INTERNATIONAL PROTECTION IN GERMANY

## ASYLUM PROCEDURE

The authority responsible for processing asylum applications in Germany is the **BAMF** (*Bundesamt für Migration und Flüchtlinge* — Federal Office for Migration and Refugees).

### Main steps

1. **Registration:** Upon arrival, you are registered at an initial reception centre (*Erstaufnahmeeinrichtung*). Your fingerprints and personal data are taken.
2. **Formal application:** You are given an appointment at BAMF to file your application. You have the right to an interpreter.
3. **Temporary residence permit:** Once the application is filed, you receive a document confirming your right to stay in Germany during the process and access to basic services.
4. **Jurisdiction determination:** BAMF checks whether Germany is the responsible country under the *Dublin III Regulation*. If another EU country is responsible, you can appeal within a few days.
5. **Personal interview:** The key stage where you explain why you cannot return to your country. You can request a female interviewer or a trauma-sensitive approach beforehand.
6. **Decision:** Issued in writing. You must keep BAMF informed of any address changes.

### Permit duration by protection type

| Type | Initial duration | Permanent residence |
|------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Refugee status | 3 years (renewable) | Possible after 3–5 years |
| Subsidiary protection | Minimum 1 year (renewable 2 years) | Possible after 5 years |
| Ban on deportation | Minimum 1 year | Possible after 5 years |

## IF YOU ARE NOT IN A RECEPTION CENTRE — WHERE CAN I SLEEP?

During the asylum process you have the right to accommodation in a reception centre (*Aufnahmeeinrichtung*). If you have no place or are homeless:

**Caritasverband für das Erzbistum Berlin** — emergency services for homeless people
- Website: https://www.caritas-berlin.de

**Notübernachtung Berlin (emergency night shelter)**
- Updated directory: https://www.berlin.de/soziales/grundsicherung/obdachlosigkeit/

> Contact KuB (see [Legal Aid](../ABOGADOS/README.en.md) section) if you have no place in a reception centre — they can guide you and accompany you in communications with authorities.

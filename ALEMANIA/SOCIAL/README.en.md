# SOCIAL SUPPORT IN GERMANY


## STATE SOCIAL ASSISTANCE

### Asylum seekers — Asylbewerberleistungsgesetz (AsylbLG)
During the first 15 months of the asylum process, benefits are governed by the *Asylum Seekers' Benefits Act*. These include:
- Accommodation in a reception centre
- Food, clothing, and hygiene
- Basic medical care (emergencies and acute illness)
- Pocket money (~€150/month)

After 15 months, access shifts to benefits equivalent to those for German citizens (*Bürgergeld* / citizen's income).

### People with recognised protection — Jobcenter / Sozialamt
Once international protection is granted, you can apply for support at the **Jobcenter** (for those who can work) or the **Sozialamt** (municipal social welfare office) depending on your situation.

- Jobcenter directory: https://www.arbeitsagentur.de/en/
- Information on Bürgergeld: https://www.bmas.de/EN/Social/Basic-income-support/basic-income-support.html

**LIFE Initiative — Help with social benefits**
- Free individual online advice for people on low incomes, including communication with the Jobcenter or Sozialamt
- Website: https://life-initiative.org/en/sozialleistungen/


## FREE FOOD

**Tafel Deutschland — Food banks**
- National network of over 900 free food distribution points for people in need, including asylum seekers and migrants
- Directory of nearest points: https://www.tafel.de/infos-hilfe/tafeln-in-deutschland/

**Berliner Tafel**
- Website: https://www.berliner-tafel.de
- Distribution points in Berlin: https://www.berliner-tafel.de/berliner-tafel/ausgabestellen/


## WOMEN: GENDER-BASED VIOLENCE AND SUPPORT

### Helplines

| Service | Number | Description |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Violence against women | **116 016** | Free, 24h, available in 18 languages |
| BIG Hotline Berlin | **030 611 03 00** | Anonymous counselling for women in Berlin, with translation |
| Emergencies | **110** (police) / **112** (ambulance) | |

### Organizations

**Frauenhauskoordinierung — National Women's Shelter Coordination**
- Network of ~350 women's shelters across Germany
- Website: https://www.frauenhauskoordinierung.de/en/
- Shelters are free for all women, including refugees and migrants, with no initial documentation required.

> Migrant victims of domestic violence can have deportation proceedings suspended and obtain a residence permit under Section 25 of the *Aufenthaltsgesetz* (Residence Act).

**Berliner Krisentelefon (women and girls in crisis)**
- Phone: 030 390 63 00 (24h)


## CHILDREN: RIGHT TO EDUCATION

**All school-age children have the right to education in Germany, regardless of immigration status. Schooling is also compulsory.**

- Children from asylum-seeking families are assigned to public schools from the reception centre.
- Welcome classes (*Willkommensklassen* in Berlin, *Vorbereitungsklassen* in other states) exist for newly arrived children who do not speak German.
- More information: https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/living-in-germany/family/children-school


## HOUSING: TRANSITION AFTER ASYLUM

Once international protection is granted, you must leave the reception centre within a timeframe that varies by federal state (generally 1–6 months). The Sozialamt or Jobcenter can support housing search.

> The shortage of affordable housing is a serious problem in large German cities. Start procedures with the Sozialamt as early as possible after receiving your decision.

- Social housing information in Berlin: https://www.berlin.de/wohnen/wohnraumfoerderung/
- Housing guide (in multiple languages): https://handbookgermany.de/en/housing

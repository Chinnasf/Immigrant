# INTERNATIONAL RESEARCHERS AND STUDENTS IN GERMANY

> People who migrate for academic reasons face specific vulnerabilities: residency status tied to their employer or institution, social isolation, performance pressure, and language barriers to accessing help. Studies show that PhD students are 6 times more likely to experience anxiety and depression than the general population *(Nature Biotechnology, 2018)*. This section exists so you know your rights before you need them.


## RESIDENCE AND WORK PERMITS

### Research visa (*Forschungsvisum*) and residence permit
Non-EU nationals coming to Germany to conduct research or do a PhD need a specific residence permit. Responsibility for the paperwork is **shared** between the host institution and the researcher — unlike in Belgium, where it falls exclusively on the employer:

- The **host institution** must sign an *Aufnahmevereinbarung* (hosting agreement) with the researcher, which serves as the basis for the visa.
- The **researcher** applies for the visa at the German consulate in their country before travelling, or for the residence permit at the *Ausländerbehörde* (immigration office) once in Germany.

> If your institution is not processing the hosting agreement or is not providing you with the necessary documentation, contact your trade union or the *Ausländerbehörde* directly.

**Job-seeking visa for international graduates**
After completing a recognised degree in Germany, you can apply for an 18-month visa to look for work.
- Information: https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/visa-residence/types/job-seeking-visa-graduates

**BAMF — Federal Office for Migration:**
https://www.bamf.de/EN/Themen/MigrationAufenthalt/ZuwandererDrittstaaten/Forscher/forscher-node.html

## TRADE UNIONS: FREE LEGAL HELP

**DGB — German Trade Union Confederation**
- Germany's largest trade union confederation, with 5.4 million members
- Free legal advice for members on labour and social security matters (after a minimum of 3 months of membership)
- Website: https://www.dgb.de/en/

**ver.di — United Services Union** *(includes academic and research workers)*
- Website: https://www.verdi.de

**GEW — Education and Science Union** *(specific to teachers, researchers, and students)*
- Website: https://www.gew.de
- Especially relevant for doctoral researchers with university contracts

> Unions operate primarily in German. You can request interpreter support or bring someone you trust. DAAD (see below) can also point you to English-language resources.


## DISCRIMINATION: HOW TO REPORT IT

### Antidiskriminierungsstelle des Bundes — Federal Anti-Discrimination Agency (FADA)

Covers discrimination based on ethnic/racial origin, gender, religion, disability, age, and sexual orientation. The service is **free and confidential**.

- Email (consultation): beratung@ads.bund.de
- Email (general): poststelle@ads.bund.de
- Address: Glinkastraße 24, 10117 Berlin
- Website: https://www.antidiskriminierungsstelle.de/EN/homepage/homepage-node.html

**FADA covers discrimination in:**
- The workplace (comments, differential treatment, dismissal)
- Educational and academic institutions
- Housing, commercial establishments, public transport

> Comments such as "foreigners perform worse" or "you don't belong here" are discriminatory and reportable. You do not need to have suffered material consequences for your case to be heard.


## MENTAL HEALTH

**DAAD — Psychological Wellbeing for International Students**
- Guide to psychological resources available at German universities
- Website: https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/advisory-service/psychological-wellbeing/

**Studierendenwerk / Studentenwerk — University psychological services**
- Every university has its own psychological counselling service (*Psychologische Beratungsstelle*), confidential and generally free for enrolled students
- Includes workshops on stress management, performance pressure, and motivation

**Ombudsperson for doctoral researchers**
- Most German universities have an ombudsperson or confidential counsellor (*Vertrauensperson*) for conflicts with supervisors or difficulties during the doctoral process
- Check your university's or Graduate School's website

**Crisis lines (Germany)**
- **0800 111 0 111** — Telefonseelsorge (24h, free)
- **0800 111 0 222** — Second line (24h, free)

**Irrsinnig Menschlich — Mental health for students**
- Preventive mental health courses at universities
- Website: https://www.irrsinnig-menschlich.de/psychisch-fit-studieren/


## INSTITUTIONAL VIOLENCE: CONTEXT

Institutional violence in academic settings — bureaucratic indifference, shifting responsibility onto the most vulnerable, comments that normalise exclusion — disproportionately affects migrants, women, racialised people, and Indigenous people.

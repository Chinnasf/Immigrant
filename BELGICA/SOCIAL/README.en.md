# SOCIAL SUPPORT IN BELGIUM


## STATE SOCIAL ASSISTANCE: CPAS/OCMW

The CPAS (Centre Public d'Action Sociale / Openbaar Centrum voor Maatschappelijk Welzijn — Public Centre for Social Welfare) is the municipal body that guarantees dignified living conditions for people who lack sufficient resources.

> **Important:** People whose asylum application is still being processed are **not eligible** for CPAS support. During that period, assistance is provided by Fedasil. CPAS applies once international protection status has been granted.

**What does CPAS offer?**
- Monthly integration income
- Housing search assistance and installation bonuses (furniture, household essentials)
- Medical cost coverage
- Employment support
- Debt mediation
- Psychosocial support

**How to access it:**
Go to the CPAS of the municipality where you are staying. Every Belgian municipality has one.

- CPAS directory for Brussels: https://be.brussels/en/assistance-social-health/assistance-individuals-and-families/cpas
- More information (in 13 languages): https://www.fedasilinfo.be/en/social-assistance-cpasocmw

## CHILDREN: RIGHT TO EDUCATION

**All children between 6 and 18 have the right to education in Belgium, regardless of immigration status.**

When enrolling a child in school, they may first enter a reception class:
- **DASPA** (French-speaking community): preparatory class with adapted teaching methods
- **OKAN** (Flemish community): reception class until sufficient language level is reached

> In practice, there may be waiting lists, especially for OKAN classes. Enrol as early as possible.

- More information (UNHCR): https://help.unhcr.org/belgium/en/where-to-seek-help/education/
- Protection of children in migration (IOM): https://belgium.iom.int/protection-children-migration


## HOUSING: TRANSITION AFTER ASYLUM

Once international protection is granted, you have **2 months** to find your own home and leave the reception centre. Fedasil can extend this period up to **4 months** in exceptional cases.

> In practice, this deadline is often too short given the shortage of affordable housing in Brussels. Contact your municipality's CPAS as early as possible for support in finding housing.

- Finding a home to rent (Fedasil): https://www.fedasilinfo.be/en/find-home-rent
- Housing First project for migrants (EU/Belgium): https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/projects/belgium-housing-first-project-benefits-third-country-nationals_en

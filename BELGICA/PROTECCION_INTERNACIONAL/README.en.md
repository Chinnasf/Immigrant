### I AM NOT IN A RECEPTION CENTRE — WHERE CAN I SLEEP?

You have the right to stay in a reception centre throughout the entire asylum procedure, including when filing a new request for international protection. Only in exceptional cases may Fedasil deny you a place in a reception centre — specifically while waiting for the Commissioner General for Refugees (CGRA) to accept new elements and declare your new application admissible. In theory this decision is taken within 10 working days, but in practice it can take several months.

Consult a lawyer as soon as possible. Your lawyer can send a letter to Fedasil's Dispatching Service and/or appeal Fedasil's decision.

Additional resources for lawyers:
- [Information on international protection and regularisation procedures in Belgium](https://vluchtelingenwerk.be/publicatie/informatie-over-procedures-internationale-bescherming-en-regularisatie-belgie)
- [NOTE: Reception for multiple asylum applications in Belgium not in accordance with the law (PDF)](https://docs.vlaamsparlement.be/docs/biblio/opendigibib/monografie/2017/300_meervoudige_asielaanvragen.pdf?csrt=14281544586800499765)

Once your application has been declared admissible by CGRA, you can return to Fedasil's Dispatching Service to obtain a place in a reception centre.

**HOW WILL I RECEIVE THE DECISION FROM CGRA?**

You must visit CGRA regularly to check whether a decision has been made.

If you have an address and wish to receive the decision there, inform both CGRA and the Immigration Office (IBZ) as soon as possible using this form:
https://www.cgra.be/sites/default/files/formulieren/formulaire_changement_domicile.pdf

This form must be submitted to both CGRA and IBZ.

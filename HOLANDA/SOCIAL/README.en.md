# SOCIAL SUPPORT IN THE NETHERLANDS


## STATE SOCIAL ASSISTANCE

### Asylum seekers — RVA
During the procedure, COA covers accommodation, food, clothing, medical access, and a basic daily allowance. More information: https://www.coa.nl/en/asylum

### People with recognised protection — Bijstandsuitkering (Social Assistance)
Once protection is recognised, you are entitled to the same benefits as any legal resident. Apply at the **gemeente** (municipality) where you live.

- Information on social benefits: https://www.government.nl/topics/social-assistance
- For people with international protection: https://asylumineurope.org/reports/country/netherlands/content-international-protection/social-welfare/


## FREE FOOD

**Voedselbank Amsterdam — Amsterdam Food Bank**
- Address: Archangelkade 11, 1013 BE Amsterdam
- Phone: 020 638 44 77
- Email: info@voedselbank.org
- Website: https://voedselbank.amsterdam/english/
- Free food packages for families who cannot make ends meet. Requires an intake process with basic documentation (ID and 3 months of bank statements).

**National Food Bank Network (179 points nationwide)**
- Website: https://www.voedselbankennederland.nl/
- Directory of nearby points: https://www.voedselbankennederland.nl/voedselbankennederland/vind-een-voedselbank/


## WOMEN: GENDER-BASED VIOLENCE AND SUPPORT

### Helplines

| Service | Number | Description |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Veilig Thuis | **0800 2000** | Domestic violence and child abuse — free, 24h |
| Emergencies | **112** | Police / ambulance |

### Organizations

**Blijf Groep — Amsterdam**
- Crisis shelter for victims of domestic violence
- Address: Tollensstraat 59a, 1053 RS Amsterdam
- 24h crisis line: see website
- Website: https://www.blijfgroep.nl/
- Serves women with or without children, including migrants and refugees, with no initial documentation required.

> Migrant women who are victims of domestic violence can apply for an independent residence permit. Consult VluchtelingenWerk or an immigration lawyer for specific guidance.

**SAMEN — Culturally sensitive support for migrant GBV victims**
- Teams of cultural mediators and medical professionals in Amsterdam and 5 other cities
- Psychosocial support in the native language of migrant communities
- Website: https://iom-nederland.nl/en/migrant-inclusion/a-migrant-centric-approach-to-addressing-gender-based-violence


## CHILDREN: RIGHT TO EDUCATION

**All school-age children have the right to education in the Netherlands, regardless of immigration status.**

- Newly arrived children who do not speak Dutch can enter reception classes (*Internationale Schakelklas*, ISK) before integrating into the regular system.
- More information: https://www.government.nl/topics/asylum-policy/reception-asylum-seekers/education-asylum-seekers


## HOUSING: TRANSITION AFTER ASYLUM

After receiving protection status, you are entitled to social housing. However, waiting lists are long in cities like Amsterdam. The municipality (*gemeente*) has a legal obligation to assign you housing within a reasonable time.

> Start procedures with your gemeente as early as possible after receiving your decision.

- Social housing in Amsterdam: https://www.amsterdam.nl/en/housing/social-housing/
- Housing guide for refugees: https://handbookholland.nl/en/housing

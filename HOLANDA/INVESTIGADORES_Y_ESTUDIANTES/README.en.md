# INTERNATIONAL RESEARCHERS AND STUDENTS IN THE NETHERLANDS

> People who migrate for academic reasons face specific vulnerabilities: residency status tied to their employer or institution, social isolation, performance pressure, and language barriers to accessing help. Studies show that PhD students are 6 times more likely to experience anxiety and depression than the general population *(Nature Biotechnology, 2018)*. This section exists so you know your rights before you need them.


## RESIDENCE AND WORK PERMITS

### Kennismigrant — Highly Skilled Migrant Permit
The main permit for researchers and doctoral candidates with a contract at a Dutch institution. **The responsibility for applying lies with the employer**, who must be an *erkend referent* (recognised sponsor) with the IND.

The employer must:
- Process the permit with the IND before the contract starts
- Renew it before it expires

> If your employer fails to manage the permit or shifts the responsibility to you, that is incorrect. You can contact the IND directly or a trade union for advice.

**2026 salary thresholds (Kennismigrant):**
- Over 30 years old: €5,008/month gross
- Under 30 years old: €3,672/month gross
- Master's/PhD graduates from top-200 universities: €2,738/month gross
- Scientific researchers: Dutch minimum wage (no additional threshold)

**IND — Kennismigrant:** https://ind.nl/en/residence-permits/work/highly-skilled-migrant
**Business.gov.nl:** https://business.gov.nl/coming-to-the-netherlands/permits-and-visa/residence-permit-for-highly-skilled-migrant/

### Orientation Year for Graduates (*Zoekjaar*)
After completing studies or a PhD in or outside the Netherlands, you can apply for a 1-year permit to look for work. Valid up to 3 years after graduation.

- More information: https://ind.nl/en/residence-permits/work/residence-permit-for-orientation-year
- Conditions and process: https://business.gov.nl/coming-to-the-netherlands/permits-and-visa/residence-permit-for-orientation-year/

### EURAXESS Netherlands
Information and support for researchers relocating to the Netherlands, including permits, visas, and labour rights.
- Website: https://www.euraxess.nl/netherlands/information-researchers/work-permit


## TRADE UNIONS: FREE LEGAL HELP

**FNV — National Federation of Trade Unions**
- The largest trade union in the Netherlands
- Free legal advice for members on labour matters
- Website: https://www.fnv.nl/

**CNV — National Federation of Christian Trade Unions**
- Website: https://www.cnv.nl/

> Unions operate primarily in Dutch. You can request interpreter support. NUFFIC and EURAXESS can also guide you in English.


## DISCRIMINATION: HOW TO REPORT IT

### College voor de Rechten van de Mens — Netherlands Institute for Human Rights

Covers discrimination in employment, education, and consumer matters. Issues non-binding decisions on individual cases. The service is **free**.

- Phone: +31 (0)30 888 3 888
- Email: info@mensenrechten.nl
- Complaint form: https://complaintform.mensenrechten.nl
- Website: https://www.mensenrechten.nl/english

> Discriminatory comments from supervisors or professors (based on origin, nationality, gender) are reportable. You do not need to have suffered material consequences for your case to be heard.


## MENTAL HEALTH

**University student psychology services**
- Every Dutch university has a student psychology service (*studentenpsycholoog*), confidential and generally free for enrolled students
- Check your university's website or *studentendesk*

**NUFFIC — Support for international students**
- Information and guidance for international students and researchers in the Netherlands
- Website: https://www.studyinnl.org/

**Crisis lines (Netherlands)**
- **0800 0113** — Stichting 113 (suicide prevention, free, 24h)
- **Chat:** https://www.113.nl/


## INSTITUTIONAL VIOLENCE: CONTEXT

Institutional violence in academic settings disproportionately affects migrants, women, racialised people, and Indigenous people.

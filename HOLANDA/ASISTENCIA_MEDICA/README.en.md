# FREE MEDICAL ASSISTANCE IN THE NETHERLANDS

## MEDICAL RIGHTS BY IMMIGRATION STATUS

- **Asylum seekers:** Access to medical care during the asylum procedure, coordinated by COA (*Centraal Orgaan opvang asielzoekers* — Central Agency for the Reception of Asylum Seekers).
- **Undocumented people:** The Netherlands is one of the few EU countries where undocumented migrants have the right to "medically necessary care" — not just emergencies. If they cannot pay, healthcare providers can claim reimbursement from the state through the CAK system.
- **Recognised protection:** Access to the public health system like any resident, with an obligation to take out health insurance (*zorgverzekering*).

## FREE MEDICAL SERVICES IN AMSTERDAM

**Wereldhuis (World House) — Amsterdam**
- Medical consultations for undocumented people
- Coordinates appointments with doctors in Amsterdam and explains the CAK system to both professionals and patients
- Website: https://www.wereldhuisamsterdam.nl/

**GGD Amsterdam — Municipal Public Health**
- Offers free medical screening for homeless people and undocumented migrants through municipal services
- Website: https://www.ggd.amsterdam.nl/
- For people in irregular situations in Amsterdam: see the Loket Ongedocumenteerden (see [Legal Aid](../ABOGADOS/README.en.md) section)

**National directory of medical care for migrants:**
- https://w2eu.info/en/countries/netherlands/medical

## EMERGENCIES

**CALL `112` FOR AN AMBULANCE**

Everyone has the right to emergency care in the Netherlands, regardless of immigration status or ability to pay.

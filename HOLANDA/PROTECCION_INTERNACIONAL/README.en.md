# INTERNATIONAL PROTECTION IN THE NETHERLANDS

## ASYLUM PROCEDURE

The two key authorities are:
- **IND** (*Immigratie- en Naturalisatiedienst*) — assesses asylum applications and decides whether you can stay.
- **COA** (*Centraal Orgaan opvang asielzoekers*) — manages accommodation during the process.

### Main steps

1. **Registration at Ter Apel:** The procedure begins at the Ter Apel registration centre. From June 2026, IND is responsible for initial registration and screening there.
2. **Transfer to a reception centre:** COA assigns accommodation, basic resources, and guidance.
3. **Interview with IND:** You explain why you cannot return to your country. You have the right to an interpreter.
4. **Decision:** Issued in writing. You can appeal to the administrative court if it is negative.

> **Important 2026 change:** The asylum residence permit, previously valid for 5 years, is reduced to **3 years** under the new European Asylum and Migration Pact (in force from June 2026).

## IF YOU HAVE NO ACCOMMODATION

**Loket Ongedocumenteerden — Amsterdam**
24-hour emergency shelter, pocket money, and medical screening for undocumented people or those whose applications have been rejected in Amsterdam.
- Website: https://www.amsterdam.nl/en/refugees/provisions-asylum/

**ASKV — Refugee Support Amsterdam**
Support for rejected applicants to access their basic rights.
- Website: https://www.askv.nl/en/

**Vluchtelingen in de Knel**
Defends access to basic rights for rejected applicants.
- Website: https://vluchtelingenindeknelnederland.nl/


## PSYCHOLOGICAL SUPPORT

**Pharos — Centre of Expertise on Health for Migrants and Refugees**
- National reference organisation on migrant and refugee health in the Netherlands
- Website: https://www.pharos.nl/english/

**PSY-COPE — Psychological support for people in the asylum system**
- Specialised trauma support for asylum seekers and refugees
- Website: https://www.psycope.nl/

# RESEARCH AND LITERATURE

This section compiles academic references, institutional reports, and analytical resources relevant to understanding migration from the Global South to Europe — with emphasis on structural inequalities, rights, mental health, and institutional violence.

References are organised by theme. Language, open-access status, and a note on relevance to this repository are indicated for each entry.

---

## FAMILY REUNIFICATION AND INEQUALITIES IN MIGRATION POLICY

**Desmet, E., Belloni, M., Vanheule, D., Güdük, A. & Verhellen, J. (Eds.) (2024).**
*Family Reunification in Europe: Exposing Inequalities.*
Routledge. https://doi.org/10.4324/9781003503217
- Language: English
- Access: Partial (some chapters open access via OAPEN)
- Relevance: Multidisciplinary analysis of family reunification laws, policies, and practices across the EU. Covers structural inequalities, the case of undocumented refugees, and the role of administrative citizenship. Directly applicable to the Belgium sections and to countries yet to be developed.

---

## MENTAL HEALTH IN DOCTORAL AND ACADEMIC RESEARCH

**Evans, T. M., Bira, L., Gastelum, J. B., Weiss, L. T. & Vanderford, N. L. (2018).**
*Evidence for a mental health crisis in graduate education.*
Nature Biotechnology, 36, 282–284. https://doi.org/10.1038/nbt.4089
- Language: English
- Access: Open
- Relevance: Landmark study documenting that PhD students are 6 times more likely to experience anxiety and depression than the general population. Empirical basis for the [Researchers and Students](../BELGICA/INVESTIGADORES_Y_ESTUDIANTES/README.en.md) section.

## INSTITUTIONAL VIOLENCE

**Leguil, C. (2003).**
*Violence de l'institution.*
Études, 399. https://www.em-consulte.com/article/19491/violence-de-l-institution
- Language: French
- Access: Open
- Relevance: Analysis of how institutions exercise violence not always intentionally, through bureaucratic indifference and shifting responsibility onto the most vulnerable. Theoretical framework for understanding institutional abuse in migratory and academic contexts.

**Bourdieu, P. (1989).**
*La noblesse d'État: Grandes écoles et esprit de corps.*
Les Éditions de Minuit. *(English translation: The State Nobility, Polity Press, 1996)*
- Language: French / English
- Access: Library / purchase
- Relevance: Seminal analysis of how academic institutions reproduce and legitimise social inequalities. Foundation for understanding why people from underrepresented groups face structural barriers in academia.
